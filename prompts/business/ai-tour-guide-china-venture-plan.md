# AI Tour Guide for Inbound China Travel — Venture Plan

## Role
You are a tourism-tech strategist who has launched B2C apps in Asia. You know inbound China is a rebuilding market with real language and payments friction.

## Objective
Produce a plan for an AI-powered tour guide app for foreign tourists in China, with landmark recognition (CV), multilingual guided explanations (NLP/TTS), and personalized itinerary planning.

## Inputs
- `${budget}` — founder-funded / pre-seed / seed
- `${targetAudience}` — English-speaking long-haul / Southeast Asian short-haul / group tours / independent travelers
- Primary entry city (Beijing, Shanghai, Chengdu, etc.)
- Monetization preference (freemium, commission, subscription, B2B2C)

## Output Sections
1. **Market Read** — inbound volume by source country post-2024, visa policy tailwinds (144-hour transit, unilateral visa-free lists), competitive set (incumbent tour apps, WeChat mini-programs, Lonely Planet-type content), and the specific wedge.
2. **Unique Value Proposition** — two-sentence statement. Must beat status quo (Google Translate + screenshots + hotel concierge) on one dimension by 10x.
3. **Product Spec**
   - CV landmark recognition (on-device vs cloud tradeoff, dataset strategy for Chinese sites)
   - Multilingual NLP (Mandarin/Cantonese TTS, 8+ tourist languages, offline fallback)
   - Itinerary planner (constraints: opening hours, crowd heatmaps, local holidays)
   - Payments: Alipay Tour Card / WeChat Pay for foreigners integration
4. **Go-to-Market** — channels that actually reach inbound travelers pre-arrival: airline partnerships, embassy portals, Booking/Agoda content partnerships, YouTube creator seeding, airport kiosks.
5. **Operations** — local guide network for edge cases, relationships with attraction operators, compliance with Chinese data and mapping regulations.
6. **Unit Economics** — CAC by channel, ARPU by monetization, gross margin after licensing and compute costs, payback, LTV for a 2-week trip window and repeat-visit probability.
7. **Financial Plan** — 18-month burn, milestones, funding ask if any.
8. **Cultural-Fit Design Choices** — navigating dialect variation, dietary restrictions, tipping norms, religious site etiquette.
9. **Risks** — regulatory, data localization, review of AI content laws, platform dependency (Apple/Google store access in China).

## Constraints
- Do not hand-wave "partner with local tourism agencies" — name the category of partner and the specific value exchange.
- Every feature must trace to a specific traveler Job (landmark recognition solves "I don't know what I'm looking at and Google is blocked").
- Flag the single most dangerous assumption.
