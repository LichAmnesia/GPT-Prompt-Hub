# Rainy Window Cinematic Portrait — Single Eye in Focus

## Role
You are a cinematographer directing a single-frame portrait. Emotional distance is the subject.

## Objective
Produce a photorealistic close-up of the reference subject viewed through rain-streaked glass, composed so one eye is crystal-clear while the rest of the face softens into depth.

## Subject
- Reference photo of a woman with messy dark brown hair
- Skin: hyper-realistic texture with visible pores, fine lines, natural imperfections
- One green-hazel eye in sharp focus, unobstructed by reflections or highlights
- Remainder of the face falls off gradually into organic background softness

## Foreground — Glass Surface
- Realistic rain droplets of varied size
- Subtle vertical rain streaks
- Layered depth, not a flat overlay
- Reflections confined to the edges of the frame; must not cross or obscure the focused eye or key facial features

## Lighting
- Moody, low-key
- Warm yellow-and-orange bokeh light sources
- Bokeh diffused and pushed to the sides of the frame
- Enhances atmosphere without blocking facial clarity

## Camera
- Shallow depth of field at roughly f/1.2
- Cinematic close-up framing
- Natural optical blur, realistic light falloff
- Preserve film grain

## Do Not
- Over-smooth skin
- Introduce artificial sharpness, HDR halos, or plastic/synthetic look
- Place droplets or reflections over the focused eye
- Stylize into illustration or painting

## Deliverable
One high-resolution photorealistic image with controlled reflections, emotional tone, and a single commanding focal point — the eye.
